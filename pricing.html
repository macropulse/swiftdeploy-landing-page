How Credits Work

Credits are consumed per operation. Read-only tools are always free.

Operations that use credits

deploy 50 credits
promote 20 credits
rollback 20 credits
decommission 20 credits
generate_infrastructure 10 credits

Always free

get_status 0 credits
get_balance 0 credits
get_context 0 credits
list_projects 0 credits
write_memory 0 credits

Full transparency before every action. SwiftDeploy shows the exact credit cost in the confirmation prompt before your agent spends anything. A _credit_warning is injected into every response when your balance drops below 20%—so you're never caught off guard.

Common Questions

What happens when I run out of credits?

Deploy and promote are blocked. All read-only tools (status checks, balance queries, project context) remain free so you can always see what's running. Credits reset on the 1st of each month.

Do unused credits roll over?

No. Credits are per calendar month and don't roll over. The free tier gives 2,000 credits—enough for ~40 deploys a month at typical usage.

What does a "deploy" actually do?

A deploy runs tofu apply against your Terraform plan, provisions or updates AWS resources, and streams progress back to your agent. Each deploy costs 50 credits regardless of infrastructure size.

Can I upgrade or downgrade mid-month?

Yes. Tier changes take effect immediately and credits are pro-rated for the remainder of the month.

Is the infrastructure cost separate from SwiftDeploy credits?

Yes. SwiftDeploy credits pay for the orchestration service. Your AWS infrastructure costs (EC2, RDS, etc.) are billed directly by AWS to your own account. SwiftDeploy shows you an estimated monthly AWS cost before every deploy.
